# Power Plant Energy Output Prediction

## Project Overview

This project predicts the net energy output (PE) of a combined cycle power plant using environmental sensor readings. The dataset contains 9,568 records with no missing values, and was used to train and compare three machine learning models.

The goal was to identify the best model for predicting PE and understand which environmental features drive power output the most.

---

## Dataset

**File:** CCPP_data.csv  
**Shape:** 9,568 rows × 5 columns  
**Features:**
- AT — Ambient Temperature
- V — Exhaust Vacuum
- AP — Ambient Pressure
- RH — Relative Humidity
- PE — Net Electrical Energy Output (target variable)

No missing values were found in any column.

---

## Models Tested

Three models were evaluated using 5-fold cross validation on the training set:

| Model | RMSE (CV) | R² (CV) |
|---|---|---|
| Linear Regression | 4.572 | 0.928 |
| Lasso (alpha=0.1) | 4.616 | 0.927 |
| Random Forest (200 trees) | 3.451 | 0.959 |

**Best model: Random Forest** — selected based on lowest RMSE.

---

## Final Test Results (Random Forest, All 4 Features)

| Metric | Score |
|---|---|
| RMSE | 3.231 |
| R² | 0.964 |

The model explains 96.4% of the variation in power output on unseen test data.

---

## Feature Importance Analysis

Random Forest was used to rank feature importance:

| Feature | Importance |
|---|---|
| AT (Ambient Temperature) | 0.901 |
| V (Exhaust Vacuum) | 0.062 |
| AP (Ambient Pressure) | 0.018 |
| RH (Relative Humidity) | 0.018 |

Ambient Temperature alone accounts for 90.1% of predictive power, making it by far the most important feature.

---

## Reduced Feature Test (AT + V Only)

To validate feature importance findings, the model was retrained using only the top 2 features:

| Model | RMSE | R² |
|---|---|---|
| All 4 features | 3.231 | 0.964 |
| Top 2 features (AT, V) | 3.711 | 0.953 |

Using just 2 features still achieves R² of 0.953 — a marginal drop of 1.1% while eliminating AP and RH entirely. This confirms that AP and RH contribute very little to predictive accuracy.

---

## Key Findings

- Random Forest significantly outperforms linear models on this dataset, suggesting non-linear relationships between environmental conditions and power output
- Ambient Temperature is the dominant predictor of energy output, contributing 90% of feature importance
- A simplified 2-feature model (AT + V) performs nearly as well as the full model, which has practical implications for sensor requirements in real deployments
